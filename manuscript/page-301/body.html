At length the barriers were opened, and five knights, chosen by lot, advanced slowly into the area; a single champion riding in front, and the other four following in pairs. All were splendidly armed, and my Saxon authority (in the Wardour Manuscript) records at great length their devices, their colours, and the embroidery of their horse trappings.

"The knights are dust, And their good swords are rust, Their souls are with the saints, we trust."

Their escutcheons have long mouldered from the walls of their castles.
